What are Notion templates and how do they help with IELTS preparation?

Notion templates are pre-designed digital frameworks that help you organize your IELTS preparation effectively. Our templates include:

  • Study planners to track your progress
  • Vocabulary databases to build your word bank
  • Writing frameworks for different essay types
  • Reading strategies templates
  • Speaking practice logs

These templates save you time on setup and provide proven structures for effective IELTS preparation.

How long does it typically take to prepare for IELTS?

The preparation time varies depending on your current English level and target score:

  • For a 0.5-1.0 band increase: 1-2 months of consistent study
  • For a 1.0-1.5 band increase: 2-3 months of consistent study
  • For a 1.5-2.0 band increase: 3-6 months of consistent study

We recommend studying for at least 2-3 hours per day for optimal results. Our courses and templates are designed to maximize your study efficiency.

What's the best way to improve my IELTS writing score?

To improve your IELTS writing score, we recommend:

  • Understanding the assessment criteria (Task Achievement, Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range)
  • Practicing with timed conditions to improve speed
  • Learning high-level vocabulary and phrases
  • Studying model answers and understanding what makes them effective
  • Getting feedback on your writing from experienced teachers

Our Writing Mastery Course and Writing Templates provide structured guidance for all these aspects.

I can't access my purchased course/template. What should I do?

If you're having trouble accessing your purchase, please try these steps:

  1. Check your email (including spam folder) for the access instructions
  2. Make sure you're using the same email address you used for purchase
  3. Clear your browser cache and cookies
  4. Try accessing from a different browser or device

If you're still having issues, please contact our support team with your order details, and we'll help you resolve the problem.
